OpenDictator

Local voice-to-text for macOS

Press Option+Space, speak, and your words appear at the cursor — in any app.

Download for macOS View on GitHub

macOS 14+ · Apple Silicon · Free & Open Source

Features

100% Local

Runs on Apple Silicon's Neural Engine. No cloud, no API keys, no subscriptions. Everything stays on your Mac.

Two Speech Engines

NVIDIA Parakeet for blazing speed (~190ms, 25 languages) or WhisperKit for maximum coverage (100+ languages).

Works Everywhere

Text appears at the cursor in whatever app is focused. Browsers, editors, Slack, email — anything.

Live Preview

See partial transcription as you speak. Silence detection stops recording automatically when you pause.

Voice Commands

Say "new line", "select all", "delete that". Spoken punctuation converts to symbols.

Open Source

MIT licensed. Read every line of code. Build from source. No telemetry, no tracking, no secrets.

How It Works

1

Press the hotkey

Hit Option+Space from any app. Fully customizable in settings.

2

Speak

Talk naturally. A floating indicator shows live transcription as you go.

3

Text appears

Your words are typed at the cursor. Press the hotkey again or just stop talking.

Your voice stays on your Mac

OpenDictator is built for people who care about privacy.

  • No accounts, no login, no sign-up
  • No telemetry, no analytics, no tracking
  • No network calls after the initial model download
  • Open source — read every line
